Results in practice

As a consultant I believe the best results come when people and processes are developed side by side. I work closely with leaders and employees to create lasting improvements. Here you can read a few examples of how I have helped organisations strengthen their ways of working, reduce waste and create more meaningful working days.

Case 1

Optimisation and sustainability in infrastructure operations

Challenge: A major Danish infrastructure company faced demands for both efficiency gains and stronger ESG focus, with an annual contract budget in the range of DKK 100m.

My role: As principal consultant I led process optimisation activities, carried out data analyses and go-look-see visits at sites, and ensured reporting to top management. I worked to reduce waste in contract tendering and initiated new digital solutions.

Results:

  • Reduced the number of shift patterns from 11 to 8
  • Extended rail life through condition monitoring
  • Introduced smarter mobility solutions for technicians in tunnels
  • Initiated digital monitoring of potential hazards on bridge structures
  • Identified challenges and solutions for future maintenance of railway rolling stock

Case 2

Strengthening Lean maturity in pharmaceutical production

Challenge: Aseptic pharmaceutical production places high demands on the environment and on employees who work in or around the equipment. There is a continuous need to maintain and develop Lean maturity while facing complex quality challenges.

My role: As Senior Lean Partner I worked closely with several senior directors and their managers. I facilitated problem solving and process mapping across departments, coached employees in problem solving and trained new employees and leaders in the company Lean tools and Lean culture.

Results:

  • Reduced production monitoring call-outs through targeted work on alarms
  • Fewer environmental deviations through digitalisation and 5S solutions
  • Higher cleaning standards through leadership coaching
  • Raised Lean maturity through coaching and training of leaders and employees in production

Case 3

More customers on time

Challenge: The shift from train punctuality to customer punctuality revealed major issues with train failures and missing deliveries from workshops at a large Danish train operator.

My role: I facilitated problem solving across the organisation and worked systematically in the workshops as Lean coach in the Lean programme One workshop.

Results:

  • Standardisation reduced maintenance time equivalent to 17 FTE
  • Improved punctuality through focus on door faults
  • Improved punctuality through focus on toilet faults
